Resumen ejecutivo

The payroll data for Q4 2026 shows consistent variance between actual payroll amounts and budgets across departments, with Health Sciences and Engineering exhibiting the highest variances. While variances remain within acceptable thresholds, the trend suggests potential budgeting inefficiencies that could impact annual financial goals.

Hallazgos clave

  • Health Sciences department consistently shows higher payroll variances (November: $3,057; December: $2,018) compared to other departments.
  • Engineering department payroll variances decreased from October ($5,304) to December ($2,169), indicating improved budget adherence.
  • Administration department shows the smallest variances across all months, suggesting strong budget management.
  • Total payroll amounts are consistently above budget, with the largest gap in October for Engineering ($5,304).

Causas raíz probables

  • Potential underestimation of headcount FTE requirements in budget planning for Health Sciences and Engineering departments.
  • Inconsistent overtime allocation across departments, particularly in Health Sciences where overtime is 2.2% of payroll (vs. 1.8% in Engineering).
  • Possible lack of real-time budget monitoring tools leading to delayed adjustments in payroll planning.
